Pasta

Description

Pasta is a staple food made from durum wheat flour and water, typically shaped into various forms such as tubes, ribbons, or shells. It's boiled until al dente, then often served with sauces, vegetables, meats, or cheeses, making it a versatile and satisfying dish enjoyed worldwide.

Ingredients

  • Pasta noodles (such as spaghetti, penne, or fettuccine)
  • Water
  • Salt (for seasoning the water)
  • Optional: Olive oil (to prevent sticking)
  • Sauce (such as marinara, Alfredo, or pesto)
  • Optional: Additional toppings or ingredients (such as grated cheese, herbs, or vegetables)

Steps

  1. Fill a large pot with water, leaving enough room for the pasta to expand while cooking.
  2. Place the pot on the stove and bring the water to a rolling boil over high heat.
  3. Once the water is boiling, add salt to season the water. Use about 1-2 tablespoons of salt per gallon of water.
  4. Optional: Add a splash of olive oil to the boiling water to help prevent the pasta from sticking together.
  5. Add the pasta noodles to the boiling water, stirring gently to prevent sticking.
  6. Cook the pasta according to package instructions or until al dente, which means the pasta is cooked through but still firm to the bite.
  7. When the pasta is done, carefully drain it in a colander placed in the sink.
  8. Transfer the drained pasta back to the pot or a serving dish.
  9. Top the pasta with your desired sauce, such as marinara, Alfredo, or pesto, and toss gently to coat.
  10. Optional: Add any additional toppings or ingredients, such as grated cheese, herbs, or vegetables.
  11. Serve the pasta hot and enjoy!
